Rules & Regulations

Eligibility: Who Can Apply?

Teams of 1–4 members from anywhere around the world. We welcome students, researchers, and industry professionals with expertise in privacy, security, ML, or data engineering.

Contest Details & Timeline

The contest has a total duration of 50 days.

  • Official Kick-off: August 1st, 2025
  • Application Deadline: September 10th, 2025
  • Mission Completion Deadline: September 19, 2025
  • Winners Notified by: October 25th, 2025

Successful teams will be invited to a hybrid award ceremony. A limited need-basis travel budget is available upon request.

Missions & Tracks

Participants will design and execute privacy attacks through 5 missions across 3 tracks of varying difficulty. Missions include database membership detection, sensitive attribute inference, record reconstruction, record linkage, and an innovative Open Mission.

Selection Criteria

Success is measured by a comprehensive scoring matrix. The only subjective area is the "Open Ended Exploration" mission. Scoring will consider:

  1. Type of attack and privacy compromise achieved.
  2. Attack metrics (e.g., TPR, FPR, AUC, MSE).
  3. Attack detectability.
  4. Attack complexity.
  5. Attacker's strength and prior knowledge assumed.
  6. Attack robustness against different privacy protection mechanisms.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can solo applicants apply?

Yes! Individuals are welcome, but teams of 2–4 are encouraged to ensure a diversity of expertise.

Are non-academic teams eligible?

Absolutely—industry professionals and independent researchers are welcome.

What datasets will we use?

The competition will use a combination of synthetic and public real-world data.
