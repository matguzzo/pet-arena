Capture The Flag: Privacy Attack Challenge

A hands-on CTF competition to design and execute novel privacy attacks in an interactive database setting.

Apply Now – Deadline: Sept 10, 2025

Objective

The objective of this competition is to break privacy protection mechanisms (e.g., differential privacy) with various techniques, including machine learning. Participants will gain access to a simulated Database System (DBS) with various static and evolving datasets through a query client, assuming the role of a malicious analyst.

Prizes

  • 1st Place: $10,000 USD
  • 2nd Place: $5,000 USD
  • 3rd Place: $2,000 USD
  • 4th Place: $1,000 USD
  • 5th Place: $500 USD
  • Honorable Mentions
  • Certificates for participants who complete at least one mission.

How to Apply

We invite any privacy, security, machine learning, and/or data expert, researcher, and engineer to participate. We especially encourage undergraduate or graduate students to apply. Submit your application via the link below. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis.

Final Deadline to Apply: September 10th 2025.

Applications should include:

  • Team bios (names, affiliations, roles) and a designated Team Lead.
  • Valid email for communication.
  • CVs of all team members.
  • Code samples or prior work (e.g., GitHub links). This is optional but encouraged.
